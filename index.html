Vinayak Sharma

Hi there! I'm an incoming MS in Computer Science student at Columbia University, having recently graduated with a BS in Computer Science and Mathematics from UC San Diego. I'm passionate about building AI systems that work in the real world — from enterprise GenAI agents and table understanding pipelines to multimodal threat detection and immersive HCI. I've interned at Articul8 AI, Scale AI, and MIT Lincoln Laboratory, and I'm currently conducting research at the Qualcomm Institute at UCSD.

I am fortunate to have worked with many professors and mentors over the course of my academic career. I conducted research in the Toor Lab at UCSD on 3D RNA structure reconstruction using NVIDIA GET3D, and at MIT Lincoln Laboratory on adversarial attacks against diffusion models, RAG evaluation, and the SocialCipher multimodal threat detection framework. As an undergrad, I also spent two years as a software developer and product manager at Triton Software Engineering, building pro-bono apps for nonprofits.

My research interests include applied AI & agents, multimodal learning, natural language processing, computer vision, and AI safety & cybersecurity.

Everything else: Outside of work, I love playing and teaching guitar, playing basketball, going to the gym, practicing karate, hiking and being outdoors, and writing about technology on my blog. I served as External Events Coordinator for TritonHacks, a UCSD hackathon bringing CS opportunities to underserved high school students.
